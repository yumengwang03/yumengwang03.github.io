Julia Sinelnikova is an interdisciplinary artist who works at the intersection of light, performance and sculpture. Her installations involve holograms, animation, projection mapping, music, and tech design that touch upon themes of digital culture and fantasy.

Sinelnikova creates dazzling immersive visions that are dreamlike, organic, galactic, technical.

Julia Sinelnikova lives and works in Brooklyn. She holds a BFA in Sculpture from The Fashion Institute of Technology (SUNY). Sinelnikova's light installations have been exhibited internationally. She has also received commissions from Norte Maar, Pace University, SELECT Fair, Webster Hall, and Good Room. She has received residencies from The Banff Centre (Canada), Brooklyn Art Space, Untitled BCN (Barcelona), KulttuuriKauppila (Finland), and The Museum of Human Achievement (Austin).

Selected profiles on her work have appeared in The Creators Project, The Wall Street Journal, VICE, Beautiful/Decay, Revolt Magazine, Huffington Post, PAPER Mag, and The New Criterion. Her work has been presented at the Contemporary Art Museum of Houston, The Oulu Museum of Art, Williamsburg Art & Historical Center, and The Vazquez Building. Sinelnikova has also exhibited site-specific installations for Miami Art Week / Art Basel annually since 2011.
